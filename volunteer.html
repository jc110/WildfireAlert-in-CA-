How to handle wildfires during this pandemic?

During this pandemic, it is important to follow safety procedures when dealing with wildfires during this pandemic.

As always, follow basic safety tips for potential, current, and future wildfires developing near you: Make family emergency plans, purchase portable air cleaner for smoke season, know how local emergency alerts function, and build supply kits.

Furthermore, during this pandemic, know the difference between smoke exposure and COVID-19. They can have similar symptoms such as dry cough, sore throat, and difficulty breathing. However, COVID-19 had additional symptoms such as fever, body/muscle aches, and diarrhea, so it is important to test for COVID-19 if you have such additional symptoms.

Overall, it is still best to practice social distancing, quarantine, and isolation even when faced with evacuation orders and wildfires near your location. Preparing sooner then later is always a better option.

For more details, read more in this link from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention:
